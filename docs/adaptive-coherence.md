# Adaptive coherence

> **Evolved thesis:** [trajectory-infrastructure.md](./trajectory-infrastructure.md) — *sustain desired human trajectory under entropy.*

**Adaptive coherence** = maintaining continuity, grounding, and coordinated adaptation under unstable contexts.

When signals are weak, timelines compress, and environments shift faster than certainty can form, adaptive systems still have to decide, delegate, and continue. Coherence is what keeps those moves connected to reality — not just momentum.

---

## What “unstable context” means operationally

- Incomplete or delayed feedback
- Conflicting authority (human override vs agent plan)
- Stale memory carried into new tasks
- Delegation chains that lose constraints
- Emotional or strategic pressure on founders
- Recursive refinement without re-grounding

These are normal operating conditions — not edge cases.

## Observable vs abstract

Adaptive coherence is not a philosophy label. It shows up as **patterns**:

- founders who reinterpret silence as failure
- grounding that weakens while agent confidence rises
- handoffs that complete without carrying constraints

**trajectory-native** collects human calibration cases. **trajectory-drift** collects system coordination failures. Same thesis — different layer.
