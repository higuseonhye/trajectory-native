# Capital-native

Understanding labor vs ownership, asset accumulation, and capital literacy — without becoming investment advice software.

---

## Core distinction

| Labor trajectory | Ownership trajectory |
|------------------|---------------------|
| Optimized worker | Asset builder |
| Time for money | Leverage accumulation |
| Consumption | Investment |
| Dependency | Optionality |
| Prestige signals | Equity and assets |

---

## Topics

### Labor vs ownership

Most people optimize for labor income without building ownership. AI accelerates this trap — making labor more efficient without increasing leverage.

**Drift signal:** Increasingly optimized worker with no assets, equity, or scalable systems.

### Asset accumulation

What you own vs what you rent:
- Code and products you control
- Audience and distribution you own
- Equity and capital positions
- Reusable systems and infrastructure
- Institutional memory and judgment

### Leverage systems

Structures that multiply output without linear time input — see [`../leverage/`](../leverage/).

### Equity mindset

Thinking in terms of ownership stakes, not just salary or hourly rates. Building things that retain value.

### Investment thinking

Consumption vs investment framing for time, money, and attention. Not stock picks — behavioral allocation.

### Capital literacy

Understanding how capital flows, compounds, and creates optionality. Avoiding moral drift that postpones capital awareness until life becomes constrained.

### Consumption vs investment

| Consumption | Investment |
|-------------|------------|
| Courses without application | Building with learned skills |
| Networking without ownership | Relationships that compound |
| Tool accumulation | Systems that persist |
| Content consumption | Content and assets you own |
| Prestige signaling | Asset building |

### Unlock the value

Identifying trapped value — skills, relationships, knowledge, systems — that could convert to ownership or leverage but remain latent.
